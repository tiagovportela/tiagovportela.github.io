I'm Tiago, a software engineer from Viana do Castelo, Portugal. My path into engineering has been anything but linear -- and I think that's what makes it interesting.

Today I'm at **Sword Health**, working as an Algorithms Software Engineer. I design real-time motion tracking algorithms for physical therapy on edge devices -- kinematic modeling, occlusion handling via dead reckoning, and concurrency optimizations that doubled throughput. I also build internal tooling with Python and have been exploring AI-assisted development using RAG-based agents.

Before that, at **Critical Manufacturing**, I shifted to industrial software, integrating semiconductor equipment into MES platforms using protocols like SECS/GEM. Working on-site with client engineering teams sharpened both my technical depth in C# and SQL and my ability to translate complex requirements into working systems.

At **Nelo**, a kayak manufacturer, I got my hands on hardware and data. I built IoT telemetry systems from scratch -- wireless sensor networks, GPS/IMU pipelines, MQTT brokers, computer vision for quality control. It was messy, physical, full-stack in the truest sense, and deeply rewarding.

I got my start at a **NOS data center**, where I discovered the power of automating the boring stuff. Writing Python and VBA scripts to cut reporting time in half taught me an early lesson: the best engineering is the kind that quietly removes friction.

Across all these roles, a few threads stay constant: I gravitate toward **real-time systems**, **applied mathematics**, and the intersection of **software and the physical world**. I care about writing code that is correct, simple, and fast -- in that order.

Outside of work, I hold a postgraduate degree in Data Science and have certifications in parallel programming (Rice University) and Anthropic's Model Context Protocol. I'm a native Portuguese speaker with professional English.

When I'm not coding, I'm probably reading about algorithms, tinkering with side projects, or overthinking the design of this website.
